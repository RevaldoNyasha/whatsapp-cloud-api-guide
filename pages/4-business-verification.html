Business Portfolio Verification

To use the WhatsApp Cloud API beyond basic testing, Meta requires you to verify your business portfolio. This unlocks higher message limits, display name approval, and the ability to message users outside your test recipient list.

You can skip this for now

You do not need business verification to follow Sections 5 through 9. Those sections work with the test number in development mode. Come back here when you're ready to go to production.

1

What is Meta Business Suite?

Meta Business Suite (previously Facebook Business Manager) is the central hub for managing your business's presence across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. It's at business.facebook.com.

Think of it as the "owner" layer above your developer apps. Your developer app is linked to a Business Portfolio inside Business Suite.

If you don't have a Business Portfolio yet:

  1. Go to business.facebook.com
  2. Click Create a business portfolio
  3. Enter your business name, your name, and a business email address
Business name must match your documents

The business name you enter here must exactly match the name on your verification documents — including capitalisation, punctuation, and abbreviations (e.g. "Ltd" vs "Limited"). Even a small mismatch will cause rejection.

2

Navigate to business verification

  1. In Meta Business Suite, click the gear icon in the left sidebar → Business Settings
  2. In the left menu under "Business Info", click Security Center
  3. Look for the "Business verification" section and click Start Verification
3

What Meta checks

Meta's verification process confirms four things:

  • Your business is legally registered (or is a recognised organisation)
  • The business name on your documents matches the name in Meta Business Suite
  • Your contact details (phone/email) are reachable
  • You have authority to represent the business
4

Choose a verification method and prepare documents

Meta offers several verification methods. The most reliable for most businesses:

  • Business documents — official registration certificate, utility bill in the company name, or a tax document (most common method)
  • Domain verification — if your business email domain matches your registered website
Common document rejection reasons
  • Document is expired
  • Business name on document does not exactly match the name in Meta Business Suite
  • Address on the document is illegible or cut off in the scan
  • Scan quality is too low — use at least 300 dpi
  • The document type is not accepted in your country (check the Meta Help Center for the accepted list)
  • You submitted a personal ID document instead of a business registration document
5

Submit and wait

After submitting your documents, the status in Business Settings → Security Center will show "In review".

Meta's stated SLA is 5 business days. In practice, verification commonly takes 2 to 14 days. You will receive an email when approved or rejected.

6

Rejection and resubmission

If rejected, Meta shows a rejection reason in Business Settings → Security Center. There is no limit on resubmission attempts — fix the flagged issue and resubmit.

Name mismatch is the #1 rejection cause

If rejected for "name mismatch", compare the legal name on your document character by character against the business name in Meta Business Suite. Update one or the other so they match exactly before resubmitting.

7

Messaging tiers after verification

After verification, Meta assigns a messaging tier. Your tier determines how many unique users you can message per 24-hour window:
