Display Name Verification

The display name is the business name WhatsApp users see when you message them. Getting it approved is slow and gets rejected often — but you can go fully live and message real users without it. The difference is cosmetic only.

1

What is a display name?

The display name is the business name that appears to WhatsApp users when your number messages them. It shows in the chat header, the contact card, and their conversation list.

It is entirely separate from your registered phone number. You must submit it for approval and Meta reviews it manually.

2

How to submit your display name

  1. Go to business.facebook.com → Settings → WhatsApp Accounts
  2. Click on your WABA
  3. Under Phone numbers, click on your registered number
  4. Click Edit display name
  5. Enter the name and submit for review
3

What Meta actually checks before approving

Meta's reviewers don't just look at the name you submitted — they look at your entire online presence to verify that the name matches a real, legitimate business. When you submit a display name, Meta cross-references:

Signal Meta checksWhat they're looking forWhy it matters
Company website A live website where the business name appears prominently Confirms the name belongs to a real operating business, not someone squatting on a name
Privacy Policy page A dedicated page explaining how user data is collected and used Required for any business using WhatsApp to communicate with users — Meta enforces data protection obligations
Terms of Service / Terms of Use Clear rules governing how users interact with your service Shows Meta you are operating a structured business, not a personal or informal account
Facebook Business Page A Page connected to the same Business Portfolio, with the same business name Meta uses the Page name as a second reference point for the display name request
Business Portfolio verification Official documents submitted in Section 4 The display name should match or closely reflect the verified business name
Consistent branding The name on the website, the Facebook Page, the verification docs, and the display name request all match Inconsistency across these sources is one of the most common silent rejection reasons
Submitting before your web presence is ready is the most common mistake

Developers often submit the display name immediately after setting up the app — before the business website is live, before policies are written, before a Facebook Page exists. Meta reviews it, finds nothing to corroborate the name, and rejects it. Set everything up first, then submit.

4

Your company website — what it needs

Your website doesn't need to be elaborate, but it must exist and clearly identify your business. Meta reviewers will visit it.

Page / elementWhat to include
Homepage Business name (exactly as you're submitting for the display name), what you do, contact details
About page Who the company is, when it was founded, what it offers — establishes legitimacy and age
Contact page Email address, phone number, physical address if applicable — Meta checks that the business is reachable
Privacy Policy Dedicated page at a fixed URL (e.g. /privacy or /privacy-policy) — see Step 5
Terms of Service Dedicated page at a fixed URL (e.g. /terms) — see Step 6
Mention of WhatsApp / messaging If your app communicates via WhatsApp, your website should say so — "We send order updates via WhatsApp" etc.
Under construction pages fail

A site showing a "coming soon" page or a domain that returns a 404 is treated the same as no website at all. Your site must be fully live and publicly accessible when you submit the display name. Meta does not wait for you to finish building it.

5

Privacy Policy — why it matters and what it must cover

A Privacy Policy is not optional. If your bot sends or receives messages containing any personal data (names, phone numbers, order details — anything), you are legally required to have one in most jurisdictions. Meta also explicitly requires it.

For a WhatsApp bot, your Privacy Policy needs to address:

SectionWhat to write
What data you collect Phone numbers, message content, names — be specific. Saying "we may collect information" is too vague and can trigger rejection.
How you use the data e.g. "We use your phone number to send order confirmations and support replies via WhatsApp."
How long you store it Retention period — "Messages are stored for 90 days" or "We do not store message content."
Who you share it with Third parties, cloud providers, analytics tools. If you use Meta's WhatsApp API, say so — "We use Meta's WhatsApp Cloud API to deliver messages."
How users can opt out How a user can stop receiving messages — "Reply STOP at any time" or a contact email to request deletion.
Contact for data requests An email address where users can ask what data you hold about them and request deletion.
Keep the URL permanent

Use a clean, permanent URL for your policy pages — https://yoursite.com/privacy-policy not something auto-generated like /page?id=43. When you submit the display name, some platforms ask for a privacy policy URL directly. A permanent, clean URL also looks more professional to reviewers.

6

Terms of Service — what to include

Your Terms of Service (also called Terms of Use or Terms & Conditions) tell users what they're agreeing to when they interact with your service via WhatsApp. For Meta's review purposes it shows you are running a structured, professional operation.

Key sections to include:

  • Who the service is for — age restrictions, geographic limits if any
  • What the service does — what your WhatsApp bot provides (order tracking, customer support, notifications, etc.)
  • What you are not responsible for — service outages, message delivery delays (WhatsApp delivery is not guaranteed by you)
  • How users consent to receive messages — important: WhatsApp requires that users opt in before you can send them business-initiated messages. Your Terms should explain this.
  • How users can stop receiving messages — the opt-out mechanism
  • How disputes are handled — governing law, jurisdiction
WhatsApp opt-in is a Meta policy requirement

Meta's policy requires that users explicitly opt in before you send them business-initiated messages. This isn't just a legal nicety — if Meta finds you are messaging users who didn't consent, your number can be flagged and your messaging quality rating downgraded, which reduces your daily message limits. Your Terms and your onboarding flow should make it clear that users agree to receive WhatsApp messages.

7

Facebook Business Page — the often-missed piece

Meta strongly recommends — and for display name approval, practically requires — a Facebook Business Page connected to the same Business Portfolio as your WhatsApp app.

Why it matters for display name approval:

  • The Page name is used as a reference when Meta reviews your display name submission
  • An active Page with real posts demonstrates your business is operational, not newly created for the sole purpose of using WhatsApp
  • A Page connected to the same verified Business Portfolio creates a consistent, cross-verifiable record

What your Facebook Page should have before you submit:

Website URL linking to your live siteCreates a traceable connection between the Page and your web presence
At least a few postsAn empty Page looks abandoned — some content shows the business is real and active
Connected to your Meta Business PortfolioRequired — a standalone Page not linked to your Portfolio doesn't help with the review
8

Pre-submission checklist

Before you submit your display name, make sure every item below is true. Submitting with gaps is the fastest way to get rejected and burn days waiting for review cycles.

  • Business website is live and publicly accessible
  • Business name on the website matches the display name you're submitting
  • Privacy Policy page exists at a permanent, clean URL
  • Privacy Policy mentions WhatsApp or messaging communication
  • Terms of Service page exists
  • Contact page exists with real contact information
  • Facebook Business Page exists with matching name
  • Facebook Page is connected to the same Meta Business Portfolio as the WhatsApp app
  • Business Portfolio verification (Section 4) is approved
  • Display name matches or closely reflects the verified legal business name
  • Display name doesn't contain restricted words (Bank, Official, WhatsApp, Meta, etc.)
  • Display name is specific — not generic words like "Tech Solutions" or "Support Team"
9

The approval reality — expect rejections

Even with everything above in place, display name approval is notoriously slow and often rejected multiple times. Here's what to expect:

What to expect
  • First submissions often come back rejected within 24–48 hours
  • Each resubmission can take 1–5 days
  • There is no appeal mechanism — you resubmit with a revised name or updated documentation
  • Meta's automated system does the initial review; rejection reasons are sometimes vague
10

Common rejection reasons and fixes

Rejection reasonWhat to do
Name is too generic Add specificity — "Tech Solutions" → "Acme Tech Solutions"
Name doesn't match verified business name Use the exact legal name from your verification documents
Name contains restricted words (e.g. "Bank", "Official") Remove the restricted word or provide proof that it applies to you
Name appears to impersonate another brand Use your own brand name — avoid anything that resembles a well-known company
Name violates Meta's display name policy Review the policy link below and adjust accordingly
11

Going live without an approved display name — it works

This is the part Meta's documentation is deliberately vague about: you can go live and message real users even without an approved display name. The API is fully functional. You are not blocked from switching to Live mode. Real users can message your bot and receive replies normally.

Confirmed working from real experience

A live app with a pending or rejected display name still receives and sends messages to any WhatsApp user. You can fully deploy and operate your bot while waiting for display name approval.

The only real limitation: how you appear to recipients

Without an approved display name, recipients see your raw phone number (e.g. +263 77 555 0000) in the chat header and contact card instead of a business name. They may also see a generic "Business Account" label.

This is a cosmetic limitation only. Everything else works: messages are delivered, the bot replies, templates work, interactive messages work.

What changes without display name approval
  • Recipients see your phone number or "Business Account" instead of a business name
  • Lower trust appearance — users may be hesitant to engage without a recognisable name
  • WhatsApp may show an "unverified business" label on the chat

What is completely unaffected

  • Ability to go Live and message real (non-test) users ✅
  • All API calls — sending, receiving, templates, interactive messages ✅
  • Webhook delivery ✅
  • All message types ✅
Strategy: launch first, name later

You don't have to wait for display name approval to go live. Deploy your bot, switch the app to Live mode, and start serving real users. Keep resubmitting the display name in the background. Once it's approved, the name updates automatically for all users — no downtime, no redeployment needed.
