Creating a WhatsApp App

Now that you have a developer account, you need to create a Meta App and add the WhatsApp product to it. There's one critical step at the end that almost everyone misses.

1

Create a new app

From developers.facebook.com/apps, click Create App. You will see a list of app types to choose from.

App TypeUse CaseWhatsApp?
BusinessPages, WhatsApp, business APIsRecommended
ConsumerFacebook Login, individual user appsNot supported
GamingMeta gaming platformNot supported
NoneMinimal app, no pre-configured productsNot supported
Select Business type

If you accidentally create a Consumer or None type app, you cannot add WhatsApp to it later. You will need to delete the app and create a new one. Save yourself the hassle — select Business from the start.

2

Name your app and link to a Business Portfolio

Give your app a name — this is only visible to you and Meta reviewers, not to WhatsApp users. If you already have a Meta Business Portfolio set up, link it here. If not, you can skip this for now and link it later.

Click Create App. You will land on the app's product dashboard.

3

Add the WhatsApp product

On your new app's dashboard, scroll down to the section labelled "Add products to your app". Find WhatsApp and click Set Up.

You will be taken to the WhatsApp → API Setup section. This page is where you do most of the initial configuration.

4

What you'll see in API Setup

Meta automatically provisions a test WABA (WhatsApp Business Account) and a test phone number for you. The API Setup page shows:

WhatsApp Business Account (Test) ├── WABA ID: 123456789012345 └── Phone Number: +1 555 140 3562 Phone Number ID: 987654321098765 Temporary Access Token: EAAVGz... [Generate]

You will use these IDs constantly. The next section explains the difference between them in detail. For now, note that both exist and that they are different numbers.

5

Subscribe to webhook fields

In your app, go to WhatsApp → Configuration → Webhook. Look for the section labelled "Webhook fields" and make sure the messages field is toggled to Subscribed.

Without this subscription, Meta will receive your messages but will not forward them to your server. You will see nothing in your backend logs even though messages are being sent.

Webhook callback URL

You cannot complete webhook setup until your server is deployed and publicly accessible. Come back to this step after completing Section 9 (Express Backend). For now, just note that this is where you'll paste your server URL.

6

The step everyone misses: subscribe your WABA to the app

This is the single most commonly missed step in all of WhatsApp Cloud API setup. When you add WhatsApp to your app, Meta automatically connects the test WABA. But when you add your own permanent phone number under a different WABA, that WABA is not automatically connected to your app.

You must connect it manually using a direct API call. This is not clearly documented in Meta's quickstart guide.

Symptom when this is missing

Messages appear to send (no API errors), your webhook shows "Subscribed" in the dashboard, but no incoming messages ever arrive at your server. This is the sign that your WABA is not subscribed to your app.

Make this API call. You can use Postman (see Section 5) or run it directly in your terminal with curl:

curl (terminal) 
curl -X POST "https://graph.facebook.com/v15.0/{WABA_ID}/subscribed_apps" -H "Authorization: Bearer {WHATSAPP_TOKEN}"

Or if you prefer the raw HTTP format for Postman:

HTTP 
POST https://graph.facebook.com/v22.0/{WABA_ID}/subscribed_apps
Authorization: Bearer {YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN}

No request body is needed. The expected response is:

JSON 
{ "success": true }

Once you receive { "success": true }, your WABA is connected to your app and messages will flow to your webhook.

Replace YOUR_WABA_ID with your WhatsApp Business Account ID (explained in the next section) and YOUR_ACCESS_TOKEN with your current access token.
